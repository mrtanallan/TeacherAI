Common questions

The stuff teachers ask before signing up.

Is my student data safe? +

Yes. TeacherAI runs on Supabase Canada Central — your data stays in Canada. Student names are never sent to the AI; only anonymized accommodation notes (e.g. "ELL Level 2") are used for personalization. Designed with Ontario's MFIPPA in mind from day one.

How is this different from MagicSchool or Chalkie? +

Three things. First, real Ontario curriculum codes — every lesson is tagged with actual expectations like C2.3 and B1.4, not generic "ELA standards." Second, split grades that actually work — generate separate worksheets per grade in one click. Third, it's built BY an Ontario teacher, not adapted from a US product. If you teach Grade 5/6 SSC in Ontario, this is the tool that was made for your classroom.

What if I don't like what it generates? +

Edit it. Every lesson, worksheet, rubric, and comment is fully editable in your browser. Tweak the wording, add questions, fix anything. Or click "Regenerate" and try again with a different angle. You're never stuck with what the AI gave you on the first try.

Does it really work for split grades? +

Yes — this is one of the things TeacherAI was built for. Pick multiple grades (4/5, 5/6, even K/1) and you get separate worksheets per grade with grade-appropriate expectations for each. The lesson plan handles the differentiation, and the auto-marker tracks each student against their own grade's standards. Works for SSCs, multi-age classrooms, and combined Kindergarten classes.

Is it actually free? +

During beta, yes — completely free, no credit card required. There will eventually be paid plans for heavy users, but anyone who signs up during beta gets locked into beta pricing when that happens. Right now I'm focused on making the tool great, not making money.

Will it work for Kindergarten? +

Yes. TeacherAI supports the new Ontario Kindergarten Curriculum (2026) with play-based "Play Invitation" lesson plans, K-specific worksheets with images, and observation-based assessment instead of Level 1-4 rubrics. Activities are designed for print-and-write — kids draw, trace, and circle on paper while you observe.
